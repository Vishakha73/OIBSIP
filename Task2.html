Ratan Tata

Ratan Naval Tata is an Indian industrialist, philanthropist, and former chairman of Tata Sons. He was born on December 28, 1937, in Bombay (now Mumbai), India. He is a member of the prominent Tata family, which has been influential in the development of the Tata Group, one of India's largest conglomerates.

Ratan Tata completed his undergraduate degree in architecture from Cornell University in 1962 and later pursued a management program from Harvard Business School. He joined the Tata Group in 1962 and held various positions within the company before becoming the chairman in 1991.

Under his leadership, the Tata Group expanded globally and diversified into various industries, including steel, automotive, telecommunications, and information technology. Ratan Tata is known for his visionary leadership, ethical business practices, and commitment to corporate social responsibility.

Throughout his career, Ratan Tata has received numerous awards and honors for his contributions to business and society. He has also been actively involved in philanthropic activities through the Tata Trusts, which support initiatives in education, healthcare, rural development, and environmental sustainability.

Ratan Tata retired as the chairman of Tata Sons in 2012 but continues to be involved in various business ventures and social initiatives. He remains one of the most respected figures in the Indian business community and is admired for his integrity, humility, and dedication to making a positive impact on society.
